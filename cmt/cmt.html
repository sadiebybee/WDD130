What is Charcot-Marie-Tooth Disease?

Charcot-Marie-Tooth Disease, also known as C.M.T is a hereditary disorder that damages the peripheral nerves in the legs and arms. It affects the nerves that send signals and sensory information to the brain and spinal cord. It can also affect the nerves that control the muscles. Usually, adolescence or early adulthood is when you may start to notice weakness in the muscles or other symptoms but the disease may manifest at any age. This disease affects the legs and arms and most individuals will have a physical disability but sometimes individuals may never know that they have the disease.

What causes C.M.T.

C.M.T. is a hereditary disease that is caused by mutations in the genes that affect the nerves and muscles in the body, especially the arms, hands, legs, and feet. There are many different genes that have been identified as C.M.T. that are all related to one or more types of the disease.

Symptoms of C.M.T.

C.M.T. affects the sensory and motor nerves of the hands and arms, and legs and feet. The muscles in the arms and legs start to become weak. Individuals with C.M.T. may have difficulty lifting the foot or drop foot, finding a good balance, and frequently tripping and falling. They may also experience foot deformities such as high arches and curled toes/hammertoes. An individual may even find weakness in the hands and have difficulty feeling hot, cold, and touch. Other common symptoms are muscle cramping and nerve pain.

More about C.M.T.

There is currently no cure for C.M.T. but physical therapy, orthopedic braces, and orthopedic surgery can help with the symptoms of C.M.T., and pain relievers can help reduce the pain. It is important for individuals with C.M.T. to receive lots of physical therapy to help with mobility, flexibility, and muscle strength.
