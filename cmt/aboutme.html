How C.M.T. has affected my life

When I was about 15 years old I was diagnosed with Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease. This disease affects everyone differently. For me, it affects my lower body, especially my feet and ankles. My hands and arms are affected a little bit but most of the muscle weakness, pain, and other symptoms are found in my feet and ankles.

I found it difficult to learn how to live with Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease. I especially found it difficult once I got to high school. This disease makes my muscles super weak and so it is super easy for me to trip and fall. Because of this, I wasn’t really able to do things that people my age should be able to do. I began to feel frustrated, depressed, and sad. What really helped me during this time was building my relationship with my family members. I was able to find a lot of comfort and support from my family as they loved me and helped me when I had hard days. My mother has MS and also struggles with similar symptoms as I do. We are able to support each other as we know what it is like to not be able to walk really well and to be in pain. I am so grateful for my family, they were able to help me focus on the good and to see the blessings that I have been given.




During this time I also focused on growing my relationship with Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ. Doing things such as going to church, reading the scriptures, and praying helped me to see God’s hand in my life. I was able to recognize my blessings and to recognize the love that Heavenly Father has for me. As I worked on growing my relationship with Heavenly Father I began to see miracles in my life. One miracle I received was that I was able to have surgery to help me walk better. After years of working with doctors and trying different things to help, I was told that I would be able to have surgery. They lengthened my Achilles tendon to help me walk better. I am now able to walk better than I was before. I am not perfect, I still trip and fall, I still find it hard to walk, and I still have days when I am in a lot of pain but I am so grateful for the blessings that I do have. My experience with Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease has helped me to see my blessings and to understand the love that Heavenly Father has for me.
